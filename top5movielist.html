Top 5 movies on my list

These are some of my favorite movies, easily among my top 5. Ranging from lighthearted comedies to gut-wrenching dramas, the genres cover everything and beyond. These films hold a special place in my heart, whether it's because of a character I resonate with, the dynamics between different characters, or the cinematography that has captivated me. You can also see a pattern in my favorite movies—I have a thing for teenage boys going through life-altering phases with an emotional, older, wise mentor who serves as a father figure (and no, I don’t have any father issues, FYI). It holds such a soft spot in my heart. In a world filled with strange, toxic masculinity, it's refreshing to see characters who are soft yet strong, wise, loyal, loving, and caring—even if they are fictional. But enough with the rambling, let's get to the list!

lets hop onto my top 5 movies!


  1. Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001)

Plot Line:

A ring with mysterious powers lands in the hands of a young hobbit, Frodo. Under the guidance of Gandalf, a wizard, he and his three friends set out on a journey and land in the Elvish kingdom.



Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring imdb page

Personal opinion

The Lord of the Rings is, without a doubt, one of the greatest films I’ve ever seen. Peter Jackson brought the books to life with such care and dedication that you can clearly see his hard work and attention to detail in every scene. All three parts are captivating in their own right, but for me, The Fellowship of the Ring stands out as something truly special.

Watching Frodo accept the daunting destiny of destroying the One Ring, Sam’s unwavering loyalty, Merry and Pippin’splayful yet kindhearted nature, Aragorn’s strength that surpasses even the mightiest of beasts, yet with a gentle heart, Gandalf’s wisdom, Legolas' grace, Gimli's fierce loyalty to those he loves, and Boromir’s very human struggles and leadership—it’s all breathtaking. The movie is far more than just a fairy tale; it tells a story unlike any other."

  1. Dead Poets Society (1989)

Plot Line:

A group of students in a highly conservative boarding school learn to rebel against the status quo and find deeper meaning in life with the help of their new poetry teacher.



Dead Poets Society imdb page

Peronsal opinion

"Dead Poets Society" is one of those films that left an indelible mark on me, not just because of its storytelling but because of how deeply I resonated with one of its central characters, Neil Perry. His journey, struggles, and desire to break free from the constraints placed upon him by his family hit close to home for me. Like Neil, I’ve experienced moments where I felt the weight of expectations pressing down, and his yearning to follow his passion while grappling with that burden was something I understood on a deeply personal level. While Neil tried to pursue what he loved, I found myself slowly slipping into becoming a less motivated student, which became an internal conflict that I’ve wrestled with for much of my life

Beyond the story, the cinematography is breathtaking. Every shot, from the serene landscapes to the intimate classroom moments, feels like visual poetry, adding depth to the emotions at play.

  1. Stand by Me (1986)

Plot Line:

Four friends, Gordie, Chris, Teddy and Vern, decide to go hiking in order to find the dead body of Ray Brower, a local teenager who was hit by a train while plucking blueberries in the wild.



Stand by Me imdb page

Peronsal opinion

Stand By Me is a film that resonates with me deeply because of its portrayal of friendship. The bond between the four teens—Gordie, Chris, Teddy, and Vern—reminds me of the friendships I once had, the kind of friends I would have done anything for. Watching their journey and seeing how they stood by each other through thick and thin takes me back to those times. The way the story ends, with the characters growing apart and moving on with their lives, hits hard. It makes me wonder how my old friends are doing now, people I would have died for once but who have become strangers over time.

The film isn’t just about a coming-of-age adventure; it’s about the bittersweet reality of growing up and growing apart. Stand By Me made me reminisce about those strong connections that once meant everything, and how life sometimes takes you in different directions. It’s both nostalgic, a reminder of friendships lost but never forgotten.

  1. Taare Zameen Par (Like Stars on Earth) (2007)

Plot Line:

Ishaan is criticised by his parents for his poor academic performance and is sent away to a boarding school. Ram, an art teacher, however, realises he has dyslexia and helps him uncover his potential.



Taare Zameen Par imdb page

Peronsal opinion

Taare Zameen Par has been a childhood favorite of mine, and it’s a movie that still hits hard no matter how many times I watch it. In many ways, it was the reason Dead Poets Society became a favorite as well. The parallels between Ishaan and Neil are striking—both are misunderstood boys struggling under pressure, each with a teacher who becomes a father figure and helps them find their voice. These connections made both movies resonate deeply with me.

Every song in Taare Zameen Par is pure perfection, capturing the emotional essence of the story. The film isn't just about Ishaan’s journey; it’s about the importance of empathy and understanding, something that strikes a chord with me every time. Its still one of the best bollywood movies ever produced. And knowing it's directed by Aamir khan, I knew it's going to be perfection from start to end.

  1. The Goonies (1985)

Plot Line:

A group of children facing their last days together before a development paves over their homes, stumble upon evidence of a pirate's treasure, attracting the attention of a family of criminals.



The Goonies imdb page

Peronsal opinion

The Goonies has become a personal favorite of mine, not just for its adventure but for the sheer silliness and humor throughout. It’s such a perfect example of great friendships, complete with that one older sibling who’s always annoyed by the younger ones. While it might not be considered special by everyone, it holds a special place in my heart as a true comfort movie.

No matter how many times I watch it, I always find myself laughing and smiling along with the gang. The goofy antics, and the excitement of their treasure hunt make it a delightful experience. It’s the kind of movie that wraps you in nostalgia and joy, reminding me of the importance of friendship and having fun, no matter the circumstances. Every viewing feels like a warm hug, and I wouldn't trade that feeling for anything!
